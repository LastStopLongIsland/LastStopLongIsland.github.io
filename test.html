About the Book

Last Stop: Long Island is a semi autobiographical tale recounting the lives of a established commuter from Long Island, New York, and his supportive wife. The book is a 25 year historical, inspirational, yet, humorous romp.

After first spending years working as hotshot bartender in Long Island’s premium clubs (where celebrity sightings were the norm and sleep depravity common), the author is reintroduced to the love of his life, his future wife Linda. Marriage and two children soon follow. Desperate to settle down, the author first tries his hand at selling door-to-door life insurance. When that fails, and through the intervention of a good friend, the author finds himself in a job in the insurance industry that 99.9% of the world didn’t know existed. Now a regular commuter on the Long Island Railroad traveling into New York City, he soon find out that in this impudent world of insurance, cutthroat tactics by high six figure salespeople was routine and undermining the competition a daily sport.

About the Author

Greg Costa

Greg Costa is a first time author and a lifelong resident of Long Island, New York. Originally from Hicksville, Greg grew up as part of a large family of ten siblings. He graduated from Fordham University in the Bronx with a B.A. in History. By the late 1970's, Greg's family migrated to the Huntington area, and he eventually moved to Northport with his wife Linda in the early eighties. As of this writing, they remain in Northport. Greg and Linda are the proud parents of two grown children, Sean and Morgan Costa.

Today, Greg continues to be a well-known personality in New York's group insurance industry. Last Stop: Long Island is the result of the urging of his fellow workers to put his story telling into writing. His second book, an audacious romance novel titled: Last Stop: Montreal, will be released in the summer of 2019.
